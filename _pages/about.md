About me
---
I am a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Oxford. From September 2023, I am a Visiting Fellow at the Kenneth C. Griffin Graduate School of Arts and Sciences at Harvard University, supported by the Kennedy Memorial Scholarship.

Background
---
My background is in pure mathematics, and I hold an MMath in Mathematics from the University of Warwick in the United Kingdom. During my undergraduate degree, I specialised in applications of mathematics to computational modelling, data analysis and complex biological processes. This led me to pursue an MSc in Mathematical Modelling and Scientific Computing at the University of Oxford. In 2021, I began a DPhil (Oxford's name for a PhD) in the application of topology - the mathematical study of shape and space - to problems in the biological sciences.

At Oxford I sit within the [Wolfson Centre for Mathematical Biology](https://www.maths.ox.ac.uk/groups/mathematical-biology) and [Algebraic Systems Biology](https://people.maths.ox.ac.uk/harrington/), and I am a member of [Lincoln College](https://lincoln.ox.ac.uk/). At Harvard I am affiliated with the [CSElab](https://cse-lab.seas.harvard.edu/) within the [School of Engineering and Applied Sciences](https://seas.harvard.edu/).

Between degrees I have worked for the [Smith Institute](https://www.smithinst.co.uk/), [Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/), [British Council](https://www.britishcouncil.org/), and [University of Warwick](https://warwick.ac.uk/).
