About me
======
I am a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Oxford. In 2023-24, I am a Kennedy Scholar and Visiting Fellow at the Kenneth C. Griffin Graduate School of Arts and Sciences at Harvard University. 

Background
---
My background is in pure mathematics, and I hold an MMath in Mathematics from the University of Warwick. During my undergraduate degree, I specialised in the application of mathematics to real-life process, in particular in mathematical modelling. This led me to pursue an MSc in Mathematical Modelling and Scientific Computing at the University of Oxford. I returned to Oxford to begin a DPhil (Oxford's name for a PhD) in the application of topology - the mathematical study of shape and space - to problems in the biology sciences.

At Oxford I sit within the [Wolfson Centre for Mathematical Biology](https://www.maths.ox.ac.uk/groups/mathematical-biology) and [Algebraic Systems Biology](https://people.maths.ox.ac.uk/harrington/). At Harvard I am affiliated with the [CSElab](https://cse-lab.seas.harvard.edu/) within the [School of Engineering and Applied Sciences](https://seas.harvard.edu/).

Between degrees I have worked for the [Smith Institute](https://www.smithinst.co.uk/), [Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/), [British Council](https://www.britishcouncil.org/), and [University of Warwick](https://warwick.ac.uk/).
